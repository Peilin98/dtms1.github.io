Inflatable Prototype

The initial prototype was built indoors to determine methodology and to forsee any potential flaws with upscaling the design.

Here are the materials we used to build the mini-prototype

  • Painter's plastic
  • Painter's tape

Our initial test determined the top of the inflatable to be the heaviest portion. If the air flow device can inflate it past this point, then the object will be inflatable. In this case, the air pump was not able to fully inflate the arch.

Here are the alternative inflation methods we tried out:

  • bike pump (not able to inflate)
  • hair dryer (45 seconds)

Air Pump Sketches and Prototype

Having done some initial testing on plastic bags before the prototypes, we anticipated needing stronger pumps.

  • piston pump
  • bellow
  • electric pump

We also DIY a bellow, created to understand air output compared to a bike pump. This proof of concept didn't hold up structurally, but from its performance, we decided to purchase a bellow, which was able to completely inflate the arch. However, the amount of effort needed to inflate the object was problematic.

On-Site Testing

On-Site Preparation

We prepared some nails, wire, bellow, and tapes to secured the mini-arch on the ground. During the preparation phase, we used bellow to inflate the arch all the way up. It takes us about 10 mins to fill all the way up. In this case, we decided to do the demonstration, and tested out the mini button that we made for the interaction portion.

Future Improvements

Our testing and learnings have informed the following improvements to future mockups, and the final product.

  • Single inflatable arch at 10 ft tall
  • Utilize electric air pumps to speed up the inflation process
  • Implement larger floor buttons with feet icon
  • May implement some other features -Cute stickers for decorating

Remaining Challenges

  • Having users engage with the buttons
  • Understanding wind and how the structure will stand
  • Overload on electric pumps
